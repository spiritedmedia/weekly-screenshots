Party with The Incline

Pitch your best story idea — and determine which stories get told here — at The Pittsburgh Pitch 2

The Pittsburgh Pitch is an experiment in crowdsourcing and crowdfunding local journalism, hosted by The Incline and The Center for Media Innovation. This event is for you if:

  • You're a local reporter, multimedia journalist or podcaster with a great story idea about the people and places of Pittsburgh
  • You're a citizen that wishes you could help decide what your local news outlets choose to cover
The goal of this project is to help local reporters — freelancers and those working in newsrooms/radio stations — produce the critical stories that people in and around Pittsburgh want to read, see and hear. We're looking for five pitches for new stories and five pitches for podcasts.
  • Send pitch ideas to cmi@pointpark.edu by midnight Oct. 15.
Here's what you can expect Oct. 27 at The Pittsburgh Pitch:
  • Shortlisted reporters will pitch their ideas in 60 seconds.
  • Your ticket gets you one vote for your favorite story pitch of the night.
  • The pitch with the most votes wins.
  • P.S. There will be beer and some appetizers. Halloween costumes are optional.
The winning reporter gets the money from the door to go off and produce the story, with the additional benefit of a pre-built local audience of people already engaged and interested in their reporting. The reporters who aren't chosen get their story idea in front of a crowd of potential readers — and perhaps a few editors and publishers.

Where: Alloy 26 at 100 S Commons (Allegheny Center)

When: October 27, 2018 from 4:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m.

How much: $10

Peculiar Pittsburgh

Find Peculiar Pittsburgh answers at this cemetery tour

Join The Incline at Homewood Cemetery for a glimpse into one of the city’s oldest cemeteries. Along the way, you’ll learn about more than 100 years of history, the famous Pittsburghers buried there, and why the cemetery is considered a “living” museum.

  • Plan for hot drinks and donuts at 10:30 a.m. with the tour starting promptly at 11.
  • Limited tickets are available, so reserve your spot now.
  • Your ticket supports our local newsroom, which is committed to keeping you informed by reporting relevant, original and actionable journalism.

Where: Homewood Cemetery at 1599 South Dallas Ave. (Point Breeze)

When: October 27, 2018 from 10:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m.

How much: $15
